PDX Solar System




Back in old Portland, Oregon, a model of the Solar System was constructed to scale. The Sun and all the planets were shown in locations throughout the city. The Sun was identified as the orange chimney at OMSI, approximately 9 feet, 1 inch in diameter. The planets and our moon were spheres of mineral or concrete. The Portland Solar System covered 150 square miles in Portland. Sites were chosen for accurate scale distance from the Sun, as well as for scenic views of the city. Touring the Solar System in Portland provided both an experience of the immense distances and powerful gravitational forces of our solar system, and a sense of the variety and beauty of Portland landscapes and panoramic views. The Sun and the first five planets could be seen on a walking tour. The remaining planets could be visited by car, bicycle, or bus. Today some of these planets remain and some are lost. This site aims to call attention to the past and encourage a revival of the Pdx solar system. Feel free to explore the locations and maybe add a planet yourself! SOL (THE SUN)

Real Diameter: 864,000 miles

Model Diameter: The Sun is represented by the chimney at OMSI. It is approximately nine feet in diameter.

Comparison: The Earth is one inch in diameter and 984 feet north on the Willamette River.

Distance to Earth: 93 million miles from the Sun to the Earth.

Facts: The largest object in our Solar System, the Sun accounts for more than 98.86% of the mass in our Solar System.

Location: The orange chimney at OMSI.

MERCURY

Real Diameter: 3,030 miles in diameter

Model Diameter: The model of Mercury is about 1/3” in diameter

Distance from the Sun: 36 million miles from the SunMoons: Mercury has no moons

Orbit: It takes Mercury 86 days to orbit the Sun

Facts: Mercury’s surface temperature can reach up to 840F. However, because of the lack of atmosphere, the temperature can also drop to -275F. The resulting 1,100F temperature swing is the greatest in the Solar System. Mercury is also the smallest planet in the Solar System.

Symbol: Mercury is a 1/3-inch diameter hematite.

Distance from OMSI: 380 feet.

Location: In this model Mercury is located 380 feet north of OMSI on a Bridge pylon along the river.

VENUS

Real Diameter: 7,515 miles in diameter

Model Diameter: The model of Venus is less than an inch in diameter.

Distance from the Sun: 67,000,000 miles from the Sun

Moons: None

Orbit: 225 days to orbit the Sun

Facts: Venus is so bright in the morning it is known as the ‘Evening Star’ or the ‘Morning Star’.

Symbol: Venus is represented by a brown Turquoise.

Distance from OMSI: Venus is 710 feet from OMSI.

Location: Venus is located 710 feet north of OMSI on a bridge pylon at the north end of the OMSI parking lot.

EARTH

Real Diameter: 7,920 miles in diameter

Model Diameter: Earth is 1 inch in diameter.

Moons: 1. The Moon is one-quarter inch in diameter, 30 inches away. That’s it, just to your right.

Distance from the Sun: 93,000,000 from the Sun

Orbit: 365 days, 6 hours, 9 minutes to orbit the Sun

Facts: You can simulate a solar eclipse with an object that is a quarter of an inch in size, like an eraser held at arms length (30 inches from ‘Venus’)

Symbol: Earth is a 1” diameter Turquoise. The Moon is a ¼-inch diameter Pietersite located 30-inches to the right of Earth.

Distance from OMSI: 984 feet from OMSI.

Location: In this model Earth is located 984 feet north of OMSI.

MARS

Real Diameter: 4,220 miles in diameter

Model Diameter: Mars is ½- inch in diameter.

Moons: Mars has two moons, Phobos and Deimos. There is no representation of them as they would be a thousandth of an inch in diameter with Phobos being ¾th of an inch away and Deimos 2-inches away.

Distance from the Sun: 140,000,000 miles from the Sun

Orbit: 1 year, 10 months, 17 days to orbit the Sun

Distance from OMSI: 1,500 feet.

Facts: The real planet Mars can often be seen as bright, reddish spot in the sky at night. It has been known since ancient times. However, as telescopes developed and more details were seen, speculation grew about the possibility of intelligent life on Mars.

Symbol: Mars is a ½-inch diameter Rutilated Quartz.

Distance from the Sun: 1,500 from OMSI

Location: Mars is located 1,500 feet north of OMSI along the footpath next to the Willamette River.

JUPITER

Real Diameter: 88,800 miles in diameter

Model Diameter: 11.25 inch

Moons: Jupiter has at least 67 moons.

Distance from the Sun: 483,000,000 miles from the Sun

Orbit: 12 years to orbit the Sun

Facts: Jupiter is visible to the naked eye and studied by the Galileo probe – the first man-made probe ever to enter Jupiter’s atmosphere

Facts/Moons: Jupiter’s four largest moons are Io, Europa, Ganymede and Callisto and can be seen through binoculars and their orbit around Jupiter observed.

Symbol: Jupiter is an 11 1/4–inch concrete sphere. To see the sun, a mile away, climb the stairs to the Burnside Bridge, about 100 yards north of Jupiter.

Distance from OMSI: One mile

Location: About a mile north of OMSI, about 100 yards before the Burnside Bridge stairs, on your right you will find Jupiter.

SATURN

Real Diameter: 75,500 miles in diameter

Model Diameter: Saturn is 9 ½-inches in diameter

Moons: 62 moons

Distance from the Sun: 483,000,000 miles from the sun

Orbit: It takes Saturn 29 1/2 years to orbit the Sun

Facts: Saturn is visible to the naked eye and named after the Roman God of the harvest. Through a telescope the rings may be seen as well as four (out of 62) of its largest moons: Titan, Rhea, Iapetus, and Dione. It is also the least dense celestial body in the Solar System.

Symbol: A concrete sphere.

Distance from OMSI: 1 ½ miles away from OMSI

Location: Willamette Park. To the left of the boat ramp, behind the “Landscaping for Clean Rivers” garden, on a tall wooden pole, you’ll see Saturn.

URANUS

Real Diameter: 31,700 miles in diameter

Model Diameter: The model Uranus is 4” inches in diameter

Moons: 64 known moons.

Distance from the Sun: 483,000,000 miles from the Sun

Orbit: It takes Uranus 48 years to orbit the Sun.

Facts: Uranus was discovered in 1781 by William Herschel using a homemade telescope. It’s tilted 98 degrees on its axis. The temperature on Uranus in the coldest of any planet in the Solar System at -224C.

Symbol: Uranus is a 4-inch concrete sphere.

Distance from OMSI: The Sun at OMSI can be seen 3 ½ miles away.

Location: Mount Tabor. From the 69th st. entrance, to the left of the parking lot of the non-vehicle road at the top of Mount Tabor. Go to the right, and you will find Uranus on the third pole, overlooking Portland.

Real Diameter: 30,500 miles in diameter

Model Diameter: Neptune is a 3 7/8”

Moons: 14 moons.

Distance from the Sun: Neptune is 2,790,000,000 miles from the Sun

Orbit: It takes Neptune 165 years to orbit the Sun

Facts: Alexis Bouvard deduced that Neptune existed based on the irregular orbit of Uranus.

Symbol: Concrete Sphere.

Distance from OMSI: 5.6 miles away

Location: Rocky Butte Park. Go up the road to Rocky Butte Castle then walk up the ramp or stairs to find Neptune.

Real Diameter:1,920 miles in diameter

Model Diameter: The model is 1/4” of an inch

Distance from the Sun: 3,700,000,000 miles from the Sun.

Orbit: It takes Pluto 247 years to orbit the Sun

Facts: Pluto was discovered in 1930 by Clyde Tombaugh. In August 2006, Pluto lost its designation as a full planet and became a dwarf planet.

Symbol: Chrysoprase.

Distance from OMSI: Approximately 6.5 miles

Location: The University of Portland. Next to the observatory in the southwest corner of the campus, there is a gazebo. There lies Pluto and Charon.
