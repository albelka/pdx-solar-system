MERCURY

Mercury Real Diameter: 3,030 miles in diameter Model Diameter: The model of Mercury is about 1/3” in diameter Distance from the Sun: 36 million miles from the Sun Moons: Mercury has no moons Orbit: It takes Mercury 86 days to orbit the Sun Facts: Mercury’s surface temperature can reach up to 840F. However, because of the lack of atmosphere, the temperature can also drop to -275F. The resulting 1,100F temperature swing is the greatest in the Solar System. Mercury is also the smallest planet in the Solar System. Location: In this model Mercury is located 380 feet north of OMSI on a Bridge pylon along the river. Symbol: Mercury is a 1/3-inch diameter hematite. Distance from OMSI: 380 feet.

VENUS

Venus Real Diameter: 7,515 miles in diameter Model Diameter: The model of Venus is less than an inch in diameter. Distance from the Sun: 67,000,000 miles from the Sun Moons: None Orbit: 225 days to orbit the Sun Facts: Venus is so bright in the morning it is known as the ‘Evening Star’ or the ‘Morning Star’. Location: Venus is located 710 feet north of OMSI on a bridge pylon at the north end of the OMSI parking lot. Symbol: Venus is represented by a brown Turquoise. Distance from OMSI: Venus is 710 feet from OMSI.

EARTH

Earth Real Diameter: 7,920 miles in diameter Model Diameter: Earth is 1 inch in diameter. -The moon is one-quarter inch in diameter, 30 inches away. That’s it, just to your right. • Moons: 1 Distance from the Sun: 93,000,000 from the Sun Orbit: 365 days, 6 hours, 9 minutes to orbit the Sun Fact: You can simulate a solar eclipse with an object that is a quarter of an inch in size, like an eraser held at arms length (30 inches from ‘Venus’) Location: In this model Earth is located 984 feet north of OMSI. It is beyond the maintenance building north of OMSI, on a bridge pylon next to the asphalt road that runs through the gravel parking lot. To see the Sun, step back across the asphalt road. Symbol: Earth is a 1” diameter Turquoise. The Moon is a ¼-inch diameter Pietersite located 30-inches to the right of Earth. Distance from OMSI: 984 feet from OMSI.

MARS

Real Diameter: 4,220 miles in diameter Model Diameter: Mars is ½- inch in diameter. Moons: Mars has two moons, Phobos and Deimos. There is no representation of them as they would be a thousandth of an inch in diameter with Phobos being ¾th of an inch away and Deimos 2-inches away. Distance from the Sun: 140,000,000 miles from the Sun Orbit: 1 year, 10 months, 17 days to orbit the Sun Distance from OMSI: 1,500 feet. Facts: The real planet Mars can often be seen as bright, reddish spot in the sky at night. It has been known since ancient times. However, as telescopes developed and more details were seen, speculation grew about the possibility of intelligent life on Mars. Location: Mars is located 1,500 feet north of OMSI along the footpath next to the Willamette River. Symbol: Mars is a ½-inch diameter Rutilated Quartz. Distance from the Sun: 1,500 from OMSI

JUPITER

Real Diameter: 88,800 miles in diameter Model Diameter: 11.25 inch Moons: Jupiter has at least 67 moons. Distance from the Sun: 483,000,000 miles from the Sun Orbit: 12 years to orbit the Sun Facts: Jupiter is visible to the naked eye and studied by the Galileo probe – the first man-made probe ever to enter Jupiter’s atmosphere Facts/Moons: Jupiter’s four largest moons are Io, Europa, Ganymede and Callisto and can be seen through binoculars and their orbit around Jupiter observed. Location: About a mile north of OMSI, about 100 yards before the Burnside Bridge stairs, on your right you will find Jupiter. Symbol: Jupiter is an 11 1/4–inch concrete sphere. To see the sun, a mile away, climb the stairs to the Burnside Bridge, about 100 yards north of Jupiter. Distance from OMSI: One mile

SATURN

Saturn Real Diameter: 75,500 miles in diameter Model Diameter: Saturn is 9 ½-inches in diameter Moons: 62 moons Distance from the Sun: 483,000,000483,000,000483,000,000 miles from the sun Orbit: It takes Saturn 29 1/2 years to orbit the Sun Facts: Saturn is visible to the naked eye and named after the Roman God of the harvest. Through a telescope the rings may be seen as well as four (out of 62) of its largest moons: Titan, Rhea, Iapetus, and Dione. It is also the least dense celestial body in the Solar System. Location: Go south on Macadam Boulevard through Johns Landing. Get off the bus or turn left at Nebraska St. to enter Willamette Park. As you enter the park, go to the right and find the parking lot and boat ramp. To the left of the boat ramp, behind the “Landscaping for Clean Rivers” garden, on a tall wooden pole, you’ll see Saturn. Symbol: A concrete sphere. Distance from OMSI: The Sun can be seen 1 ½ miles away

ISMENE

Real Diameter: 70 kilometersModel Diameter: The model of would be 5,000th of an inch in diameter. Orbit: 7.96 years Location: 4,000 feet from the Sun Symbol: A brass plate 2 inches in diameter Location: The brass plate is next to the entrance at 707 SE 12th, just north of Belmont SE. It may be covered with leaves or grass. It is about halfway to Uranus, on the north side.

URANUS

Real Diameter: 31,700 miles in diameter Model Diameter: The model Uranus is 4” inches in diameter Moons: 64 known moons. Distance from the Sun: 483,000,000 miles from the Sun Orbit: It takes Uranus 48 years to orbit the Sun. Facts: Uranus was discovered in 1781 by William Herschel using a homemade telescope. It’s tilted 98 degrees on its axis. The temperature on Uranus in the coldest of any planet in the Solar System at -224C. Location: Walk or drive up 69th st. to Mount Tabor Park( closed to vehicles on Wednesday), turn right and go up the hill to the stop, left at the stop sign, wind your way past the playground to a parking spot. Leave your car, go up the stairs to the left of the parking lot to the non-vehicle road at the top of Mount Tabor. Go to the right, and you will find Uranus on the third pole, overlooking Portland. Symbol: Uranus is a 4-inch concrete sphere. Distance from OMSI: The Sun at OMSI can be seen 3 ½ miles away.

NEPTUNE

Neptune: Real Diameter: 30,500 miles in diameter Model Diameter: Neptune is a 3 7/8” Moons: 14 moons. Distance from the Sun: Neptune is 2,790,000,000 miles from the Sun Orbit: It takes Neptune 165 years to orbit the Sun Facts: Alexis Bouvard deduced that Neptune existed based on the irregular orbit of Uranus. Location: Head north on 82 nd (72 bus) about 2.2 miles to Fremont Street, which is marked with two large green signs, one for the Portland Bible College and the other for Honeysuckles Lingerie. Go right on Fremont,(33 bus) veer right at 89th st then onto Benjamin, then right on 92nd and then to the stop sign at Russell st.. Go left up hill just past Russell st.. Continue up the hill for 1 ¼ miles, passing through the figure- 8 tunnel (hey, you’re inside a volcano for about 30 seconds). At the end of the road park at the Rocky Butte Castle, and walk up the ramp or stairs to find Neptune. Symbol: Concrete Sphere. Distance from OMSI: 5.6 miles away

PLUTO AND CHARON

Real Diameter:1,920 miles in diameter Model Diameter: The model is 1/4” of an inch Distance from the Sun: 3,700,000,000 miles from the Sun. Orbit: It takes Pluto 247 years to orbit the Sun Facts: Pluto was discovered in 1930 by Clyde Tombaugh. In August 2006, Pluto lost its designation as a full planet and became a dwarf planet. Location: Leave Rocky Butte on the road 100 yards to the right of the one you arrived on. This road will get you back to Fremont and the 33 bus. Head west on Fremont, through some recently revitalized neighborhoods. After about 5 miles, go right on Williams(bus #40), left on Killingsworth, right on Vancouver, and left on Portland Blvd. Follow Portland Ave to the end and go right on Willamette Blvd. It curves around the bluff above the Swan Island Shipyards, and turns right at the University of Portland. Enter at the main entrance, and try to find a parking spot near the bluff. Walk on the grass towards the west and find the gazebo next to the small, observatory building. Inside the gazebo is Pluto and Charon. Symbol: Chrysoprase. Distance from OMSI: Approximately 6.5 miles
